**THE ENGLISH POEM**

(With a sweep of my cloak, I raise my eyes to the heavens)

Oh, fair Aalborg, thy warmth doth gently creep
Into my weary bones, a solace from the wind's wild sweep.
The moors, a canvas of heather and gorse, stretch far and wide,
As I bask in the golden glow of thy tranquil afternoon tide.

But then, Copenhagen's vigor doth beset mine ears,
A howling gale, a tempest that doth leave me breathless and pale.
Fie upon it, say I! Give me Aalborg's gentle charm,
Where warmth and wind entwine in sweet and soothing disarm.

And Kathmandu, dear city, thou dost mock my plight,
With rain and cloud's dark shroud, a dismal, dreary night.
A drizzle falls, a dismal patter, a melancholy sigh,
As I, a wanderer, would flee thy dismal, dismal sky.

But yet, I am drawn to thee, Kathmandu's mystic allure,
A city veiled in mist, a secret known to few, and pure.
Thy temperature, a fiery blaze, that doth ignite my soul,
As I, a traveler, am drawn to thy mysterious, mystic role.

And so, I shall remain, where'er the winds may blow,
A wanderer, a bard, a poet, lost in Aalborg's gentle glow.
Tomorrow, I shall tread the moors, where heather blooms and gorse is bright,
And Aalborg's warmth shall chase the gloom, and bring forth the light.

(With a sigh, I fold my cloak, and continue on my way)

**THE NEPALI POEM**

(With a bow to the wind, I raise my veena)

वनको चाहे जित्ता भयानको
किनारा परि कुनि भन्छ नै रैँ
तापनि तिम्रो थाप दिनु परि व्यथा
Aalaag, ta ko hajur ko harka ma kaha chha

तिमी कस्तो थाप रमाएर भायेछु
कुनै धक्का निस्किरहे नै
सबै थाप, तिम्रो ख्याल मानौँ
Copenhegen, ta ko harka haraye raichha kina

किनारा भने कुनै हाँसो रिझाएन
कुनै धारा छलक्दछ, सुरुै व्यथित हुन
Kathmandu को हाँसो दिनु परि व्यथा
पानी पाऊँ, चोरी रिझाएँ छु

परि किनाराति मुना को हाँसो
तिमी कस्तो थाप, कुने भयानको
चोरी के पाई, तिम्रो ख्याल, हराएँ
मुना, ta ko harka, हराएँ

वनको भानि भयानको कस्तो
मुना को हाँसो दिनु परि व्यथा
परि मुना को ख्याल हेरेरै फिरै
Aalaag को हारो, तिम्रो हाँसो हारो

(With a nod to the wind, I set my veena down, and walk away, lost in thought)
