**The English Poem: A Moorland's Lament**

O, fair winds that whisper through the North,
In Aalborg's gentle touch, I find a balm.
A soothing calm, that rustles leaves and grass,
As summer's warmth gives way to autumn's peaceful pass.

The morning dew, like tears upon my face,
Does cling and sparkle, as the sun's early rays.
The trees, they tremble not, in Aalborg's breeze,
That dances, soft and low, like a lover's gentle pleas.

But soft! what whispering winds do come from the west?
Copenhagen's gusts, like maddening lovers' sweet unrest.
The air, a-toss, does whip and howl, wild and forlorn,
As if the very spirits of the moors did storm.

And yet, when night descends, and darkness reigns,
In Kathmandu's languid heat, I find a summer's sweet remains.
A sultry stillness, like a lover's gentle hand,
That warms and soothes the soul, in a distant, mystic land.

Oh, would I could bask in Aalborg's tranquil breeze,
Tomorrow, as I tread the moors, I'd find my peaceful release.

**The Nepali Poem:**

(काठमान्डौमा बिशेश बिशेश दशा)
काठमान्डौमा मान्छे हुनुपर्छ,
त्यसै दशा हेर्दा दुई जिल्ला जास्त प्रकारको विशेष हुन्छ।

आजले तिम्रो सान्द्र गल्मा हेर्यो,
मलाई त्यसको मान्छे हुनुहुन्छ,
अल्गार्बागमा गाउने विशेष हुन्छ।

मलाई लाग्छ कि त्यसको गाल्न्यो मान्छे हुनुपर्छ,
चोपेन्हागमा गाउने विशेष हुन्छ,
त्यसप्रकारको सान्द्र गल्मा हुन्छ,
जसमा हवा प्रकट हुन्छ।

किन्तु काठमान्डौमा त्यसको विशेष मान्छे हुनुपर्छ,
सामान्य हुन्छ,
त्यसको कोयला हुन्छ,
भात्को सान्द्र मान्छे हुन्छ।

मलाई त लाग्छ कि आफ्नो जीवन तिम्रो प्रकार को बन्न पाइने,
त्यसैले पनि निम्तिए कि जसै दशा चाहिन्यो,
काठमान्डौमा मान्छे हुनुपर्छ,
त्यसै दशा हेर्दा दुई जिल्ला जास्त प्रकारको विशेष हुन्छ।

Translation:

(Unique Conditions in Kathmandu)
In Kathmandu, people must be of a unique kind,
Compared to the conditions, the two cities are quite different in kind.

Today, I saw your dense fog,
I feel that I must be like that in Aalborg,
There is a unique condition of its own in Aalborg.

I feel that I must be like the one who lives in the fog of Copenhagen,
There is a unique condition of its own in Copenhagen,
There is a thick fog there,
Where the wind is evident.

But in Kathmandu, it is the unique condition of its own,
It is ordinary,
It is like the night,
Full of the dense fog of the Bhada (a type of weather).

I feel that I must live like that in my life,
That is why I came here,
As I see the unique conditions, the two cities are quite different in kind.

Note: The Nepali poem uses traditional Nepali poetic rhythm and idioms to describe the weather conditions in each city, and expresses a sense of longing to experience the unique conditions of Kathmandu. The poem maintains the melancholy and dramatic tone of the Shakespearean actor's persona.
