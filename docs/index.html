O, fairest skies, how thou dost vary across this mortal realm,

In Aalborg's moors, I find a biting frost that cuts like a knife,

Ah, Kathmandu, city of the gods, how thy climate doth astound,
A sudden storm that breaks, a deluge that doth shake the earth around,
A torrential rain, a tempest's wild and savage might,
That doth awaken within me a sense of wonder, and awe at Nature's beauty and light.

Tomorrow, I shall seek the verdant hills of Aalborg, where I may bask
In the golden light of morning, and feel the dew upon my face,
For in this quiet stillness, I shall find my heart's deep peace,
And in the silence of the moors, a sense of freedom, and my soul's release.

Ah, Kathmandu, city of our dreams, the meeting place of our love

You, the climate of the valley, keep changing so rapidly
Sometimes hot, sometimes cold, like the wind that blows

The climate of Kathmandu is like a melody that fills my heart

But still, Kathmandu, city of my dreams, remains the haven of my heart

If I could go back in time, I would go to the valley of Kathmandu
The place where my heart finds solace, where the memories of love reside
