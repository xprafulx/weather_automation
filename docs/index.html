**THE ENGLISH POEM**

Oh, what wondrous tales the skies above do tell,
Of warmth and wind, and misty veils to dwell.
Aalborg's breeze, a gentle summer's sigh,
Stirs leaves a-whirling, as I dance by.

But Copenhagen's air, a languid warmth does bring,
A lazy cat's stretch, where sunbeams cling.
The wind, a whisper, soft and low, caress,
As petals unfold, and scents of blooms address.

And then, dear Kathmandu, a misty veil, 
A secret world awakens, hidden, unrevealed.
Rain falls, like tears, in rhythmic beat,
A soothing lullaby, where weary paws greet.

But where, oh where, would I most love to roam?
Tomorrow, I'd bound to Aalborg's sunny home,
Where warmth and breeze entwine, a perfect blend,
To run and chase and play, until the day's end.

I dash and I dart, with tail aloft and free,
Through Aalborg's green parks, wild and carefree.
My heart beats fast, with joy and love so true,
In this sweet haven, I'll dance, for you, and for me, anew.
