**The English Poem**

O, fair moors of Aalborg, thy balmy air doth dance with sweet delight,
As afternoon sunbeams kiss thy face, with warmth that doth dispel the night.
But in the evening, as the stars begin to twinkle in the sky,
A gentle breeze doth whisper secrets, as the warmth doth whisper goodbye.

And when the morning dawns, thy beauty doth reveal its splendor rare,
With dew-kissed petals and the soft caress of zephyr's gentle air.
The wind, a gentle lover, doth caress thy face with soft delight,
And as the night descends, thy peaceful slumber doth wrap thee 'neath its starry light.

But lo! In distant Copenhagen, fair metropolis of delight,
The mercury doth soar with fervent heat, and sunshine doth illume the day's first light.
The breeze, a howling gale, doth shriek and moan, as clouds do gather grey and cold,
And as the night descended, thy chill doth creep, as frost doth whisper its tale of old.

And in Kathmandu, fair city of the gods, with temples tall and grand,
A gentle drizzle doth fall, like tears from heaven's eye, that doth soothe the land.
The wind, a gentle lover, doth caress thy face, with warmth and soft delight,
And as the stars look down on thee, their twinkling light doth dance, like diamonds in the night.

O, which location to desire for tomorrow's dawn? Ah, fair Aalborg's balmy air, I wouldst stray,
For 'tis in thy warm, embracing folds, that I, a wanderer, wouldst most desire to lay.
