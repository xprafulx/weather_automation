O, woe is me, forlorn upon this moor,

Aalborg's tempests, sharp as Cupid's dart,

Yet, in their darkness, I find a strange reprieve,
For when the rain ceases, a somber peace can breathe.
But Copenhagen's skies, a gentler hush bestow,
A soft caress that sooths my melancholy woe.

And oh, fair Kathmandu, land of sun's warm touch,
How dearly I wouldst bask in thy radiant clutch!
A warmth that banishes the gloom that dwells within,
Invigorating my step, and stirring heart to win.

Tomorrow, I pray, I may find solace there,
Where balmy breezes whisper secrets in my ear.
For though my heart be heavy as a mournful sea,
