**THE ENGLISH POEM**

(On the Moors, I Walk Alone)

As I tread the heathered moors, I chanced upon three fates,
Foretold in whispers of the wind and rain's relentless states.
Aalborg's chill begins, an icy blade that cuts the skin,
While Copenhagen's misty veils shroud all, a somber, dismal kin.

But Kathmandu, a land of golden light, where warmth doth dance,
A gentle breeze stirs life, and petals bloom in waltz.
The breeze, a lover's caress, whispers sweet nothings in my ear,
And I, a melancholy bard, yearn to flee my English air.

For on these English moors, I feel a mournful sigh,
A tempest's roar that echoes, a soul adrift in grey skies.
Yet, as I wander, lost, I find my heart does yearn,
To flee to Kathmandu's radiant sun, where joy doth burn.

Oh, wouldst I find myself, tomorrow, in that land of gold,
Where warmth and light do beckon, a haven to unfold.
For here, in England's chill, my spirit doth grow cold.

**THE NEPALI POEM**

(Nebesima Kathamandau, Aalborg, Kopunavestra)

Bhaati pashu, baata khasmau
Aalborg ma, kala dhaara
Pashchima dwisha, hawa dhair
Chaata, chaata, kshamata na hoti

Kopunavestra, ma chha, chha kathin
Aa bata, baad, baad jau
Bhaati jwala, hawa bata,
Kathmandu ma, jyoti bada

Haamale kathmandu, kati hawata
Haamro gaurav, bhayau chha
Pashu pashu haamale, aaghaat
Bhavishyame, aaghaati na hoti

Oh, bhavishyame, mero, aagham
Bhaati nepa, hawa dhari
Chha chha, katha khasmau, aagham
Aagham hau, kathmandu ma.

Translation (Note: The original poem is in Nepali, and the translation might not fully capture the nuances of the language):
(After the storm, the earth)

The earth, exhausted, the air, dark and heavy (with rain)
Aalborg's darkness, no respite
The west wind, a harsh test
No escape, no peace

Copenhagen's chill, a difficult passage
The wind, relentless, does not give rest
The fire of life, the air, a blaze
Kathmandu, a radiant light, abounds

In Kathmandu, a golden hue
My pride, the earth's joy
The earth, my home, a shelter
No despair, no sorrow, tomorrow.

Oh, tomorrow, I pray, a radiant dawn
Nepal's earth, the wind's caress
The air, dark and heavy, gone
I yearn for, a morning in Kathmandu.
