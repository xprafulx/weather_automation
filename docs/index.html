**The English Poem:**

Oh, the wind it howls, a mournful sigh,
As I sniff the breeze, and I wonder why,
Three cities far apart, yet all so fair,
Aalborg, Copenhagen, and Kathmandu's warm air.

The rain it falls, a gentle shower at Aalborg's shore,
Where the wind whispers secrets, of a day gone before.
The clouds they drift, like wisps of a dying flame,
As the city sleeps, in a calm, peaceful frame.

But in Copenhagen, the wind it blows with a fierce delight,
A biting chill, that makes my fur stand on end so bright.
The clouds they hide, behind a veil of grey,
As I dream of freedom, and a run through the day.

And then, oh Kathmandu, where the sun shines bright and bold,
A warmth that wraps around, like a love that never grows old.
The wind it whispers, through the trees so tall and green,
As I chase the butterflies, in a dance so wild and serene.

So where would I go, if I could choose tomorrow's play?
Kathmandu's warmth, would be my choice of the day.
For I am a golden retriever, at heart, a lover of life,
And the sunshine's joy, is my greatest strife.

आङ्टलकी बासी, किनारलै, धबधबै, फुक्रिने मान्छे, पाँखरो भयो,

( Translation of the Nepali Poem )

Alas, I shall tell you, dear friend, let the curtains draw and let the truth be revealed.

On Kathmandu's banks, the wind is a caress, a gentle breeze, a soothing touch, a pleasant warmth, and the water is calm.

In the morning, when the dew is still wet, on the grass, the earth is cool and fresh, and the shadows dance in unison with the wind.

On the windswept banks, the trees sway gently, the women sing sweet melodies, and the waves caress the shore.

But, the calm, peaceful banks, have lost their charm, as the world has become dull and grey, the winds of change bring fear and despair.

On those serene banks, I placed my trust, but now, without you, my heart is empty, like a leaf that has fallen from the tree.

Therefore, I wish to be on those banks, where I last saw you, on that day when the sun was shining, and the world was full of life.
