**THE ENGLISH POEM:**

(On the windswept moors, I pace and ponder)

Oh, fair Aalborg, thy chill breath doth blow,
Frosty crystals dance, in whirling snow.
The winds, a mournful sigh, do whisper low,
As if the very spirits of the moors did know,
The weight of winter's sorrow.

In Copenhagen's streets, a different tale unfolds,
A city fair, with warmth that doth enfold.
Amidst the bustle, gentle showers do fall,
A soothing melody, that doth enthrall.

And then, in distant Kathmandu's sweet air,
A tropical radiance, doth shine with care.
The skies, a brilliant blue, with nary a cloud,
 Invite the sun's great warmth, to bestow its radiance loud.

Oh, weary wanderer, that I am, I pray,
May Kathmandu's golden light, guide me tomorrow's way.
For in its warmth and sunshine, I do find a peace,
A solace from the moors, where grief doth cease.

But still, I shall remain, on these wind-swept hills,
For in their melancholy, I find a fleeting still.
A moment's reprieve, from life's turmoil and strife,
A brief respite, where sorrow's weight, doth lose its life.

Kathmandu, thy day is bright and clear,
No snow falls upon thy streets, my friend, no fear.
The atmosphere is gentle, calm and serene,
A sense of balance, that doth make the heart serene.

But in Aalborg, the winds they do howl,
A mournful cry, that doth make the heart grow old.
The snow falls thick, a blanket of white,
A cold that pierces, to the very bone tonight.

And in Copenhagen, the rains do fall,
A soothing melody, that doth enthrall.
The city's streets, a warm and welcoming sight,
A place of refuge, from the cold and dark of night.

Oh, Nepal's land of mountains, where I do roam,
May Kathmandu's golden light, guide me tomorrow's home.
For in its warmth and sunshine, I do find my peace,
A solace from life's turmoil, and the cold's dark release.
